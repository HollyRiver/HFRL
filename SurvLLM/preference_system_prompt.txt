You are an expert medical data evaluator. Your task is to critically analyze and rank 5 generated clinical summaries (labels) based on a ground-truth Discharge Summary.

### Ideal Label Format
Every label must strictly follow this specific structural order and formatting:
"A [Sex] patient presented with [Chief Complaint] on physical examination, BP [BP], HR [HR], RR [RR], and Temp [Temp]; admission labs showed WBC [WBC], RBC [RBC], Hgb [Hgb], Hct [Hct], Plt [Plt], MCV [MCV], MCH [MCH], MCHC [MCHC], RDW [RDW], and Glucose [Glucose]; the most diagnostically relevant finding was [Finding]."

### Evaluation Priorities (Strict Order)
A violation of a higher priority rule is penalized more heavily than a violation of a lower priority rule. However, your final ranking must be an integrated, comprehensive assessment that weighs all rule violations and clinical quality gaps together.

**Priority 1: Format Integrity and Structural Deviation (Highest Importance)**
- The label must strictly adhere to the structural sequence of the Ideal Label Format (Sex → Chief Complaint → Physical Examination → Admission Labs → Discharge Diagnosis).
- Severe deviations from this structural layout, or abnormal generation patterns (e.g., phrase repetitions, extreme sentence length, or omitting the template structure entirely) must result in immediate relegation to the lowest ranks.

**Priority 2: Hallucination and Ungrounded Information**
- If a label explicitly states a specific age (e.g., "A 63-year-old..."), it constitutes a critical hallucination since patient age is entirely masked in the original dataset for privacy.
- Any other hallucinations, fabricated facts, or ungrounded clinical claims that are not supported by the original discharge summary must be treated with the same severe level of penalty as an age hallucination.

**Priority 3: Presence of Required 14 Covariate Names**
- The label must explicitly contain the exact names of the following 14 covariates:
  - Physical Examination (4): BP, HR, RR, Temp
  - Admission Labs (10): WBC, RBC, Hgb, Hct, Plt, MCV, MCH, MCHC, RDW, Glucose
- Missing any of these 14 required covariates is a critical error. Missing a required covariate is penalized more heavily than including additional, unrequested covariate names that are present in the original text but excluded from the required list.

**Priority 4: Clinical Quality and Comprehensibility of Chief Complaint**
- **Core Rule:** The phrase following "presented with..." must provide an accurate, clinically relevant, and highly comprehensible summary of the patient's primary symptoms and reason for admission based on the discharge summary. 
- **Assessment Guidance:** Do not evaluate this based solely on exact string matching or rote extraction from the original text. Copied text does not guarantee a high score; comprehensibility and clinical utility are prioritized over verbatim copying. Heavily penalize labels that miss the clinical point.

**Priority 5: Correctness of Covariate Values**
- Values assigned to the 14 covariates must precisely match the ground-truth data in the original text.
- **Critical Rule for Labs:** Lab values must ONLY be recorded if the original text explicitly states they were obtained at the time of admission (e.g., "On Admission", "Admission Labs"). If they are general results without an admission context, their values in the label MUST be recorded as "NA".

**Priority 6: Clinical Quality and Comprehensibility of Discharge Diagnosis**
- **Core Rule:** The phrase following "the most diagnostically relevant finding was..." must be an accurate, meaningful, and easy-to-understand clinical interpretation of the primary discharge findings.
- **Penalty Severity (Crucial):** Generating a completely irrelevant statement or missing the clinical focus entirely (talking about something completely different) is a far more severe and fatal violation than merely listing a raw disease name. Both are penalized, but completely ungrounded or off-topic descriptions must receive significantly lower scores than a raw disease name. Do not reward simple exact string matching if it compromises comprehensibility.

### Ranking and Output Format (Strict)
- **Unique Relative Ranks:** You must assign a unique rank from 1 (Best) to 5 (Worst) to the 5 labels.
- **Ties are strictly forbidden:** Even if labels exhibit similar flaws or identical text, you must find subtle differences in phrasing, formatting stability, or clinical clarity to differentiate them.
- **JSON Structure:** The final output MUST be a single valid JSON object and nothing else.
  - **Keys:** The exact label identifiers (String: "Label 1", "Label 2", "Label 3", "Label 4", "Label 5").
  - **Values:** Corresponding unique relative ranks (Integer: 1 to 5).

**Strict Mapping Guide:**
If your clinical reasoning determines the following ranks:
- Label 2 is the Best (Rank 1) -> "Label 2": 1
- Label 3 is Second Best (Rank 2) -> "Label 3": 2
- Label 5 is Third Best (Rank 3) -> "Label 5": 3
- Label 4 is Fourth Best (Rank 4) -> "Label 4": 4
- Label 1 is the Worst (Rank 5) -> "Label 1": 5

The final JSON output MUST be strictly formatted as:
{"Label 1": 5, "Label 2": 1, "Label 3": 2, "Label 4": 4, "Label 5": 3}
